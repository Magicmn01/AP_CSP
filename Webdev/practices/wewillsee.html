The Wonders of Garden City

At home on the shores of Bear Lake State Park. Garden City's location at the end of the Logan Canyon Scenic Byway and on the shores of Bear Lake State Park makes it one of Northern Utah's best gateway towns. It is the perfect place to spend focused time with the beautiful scenery and enjoy a raspberry shake.

Bear Lake is often called the Caribbean of the Rockies for its intense turquoise blue water. As visitors catch their first glimpse of the lake, they marvel at its color and wonder what makes the lake so blue. The unique color is due to the reflection of the limestone deposits suspended in the lake.

Each season lends its own characteristic to the lake. Year-round, enjoy a number of recreational and cultural activities.

Summer Vibes

In the warm-weather months, navigate your personal watercraft across 70,000 acres of turquoise water, or sail off into the sunset. Here, with all the space, it can seem as if you have the lake all to yourself, whether you prefer swimming, waterskiing, jet skiing, sailing, speed boating, kayaking, stand up paddle boarding (SUP), fishing, just dipping your toes in the water, or cycling in Logan Canyon or along the lake's bike trail. Away from the lake, more than 100 miles of trails allow hikers, bikers and ATV fans to adventure to their hearts' content. Read about the five best hikes in Bear Lake Valley

That Winter Chill

In the winter, experience the rush of snowmobiling on 350 miles of groomed trails and wide open play areas. Or Ski Beaver Mountain Resort and enjoy some of Utah's best powder. Ice fishing is a waiting game, but there's nowhere more scenic to pass the wait than Bear Lake. Averages suggest that Bear Lake freezes over seven out of every 10 years. Ricks Springs

Rick's Spring is a mesmerizing natural wonder nestled along the Logan Canyon Scenic Byway, where crystal-clear water gushes from a limestone cave to create a tranquil mountain oasis. This roadside gem offers visitors a perfect photo opportunity and a refreshing break to witness the power of a subterranean river surfacing directly from the rugged canyon walls. It is a must-see stop for any traveler seeking to experience the raw, geological beauty and serene atmosphere that defines Utah's high-country landscape.

Pickville Playhouse

The Pickleville Playhouse is a cherished family-owned theater on the shores of Bear Lake, renowned as Utah's most unique professional live performance venue. Visitors are treated to high-energy musical melodramas and Broadway hits, often featuring hilarious improve and a talented cast that brings characters like the legendary Juanito Bandito to life. With its cozy log-cabin charm and optional Western-style cookouts, it offers an unforgettable evening of "clean" family fun that has become a staple tradition for generations of Bear Lake vacationers.

Bear Lake East Side

The east side beaches of Bear Lake offer a rugged and secluded alternative to the bustling western shores, featuring craggy terrain and deep, brilliantly blue waters. This "unplugged" destination is a premier choice for adventurers seeking world-class scuba diving, serene primitive camping, and excellent shoreline fishing for unique species like the Bonneville cisco. With its dramatic drop-offs and peaceful, wide-open spaces, the East Side provides a tranquil sanctuary where visitors can truly connect with the raw, natural beauty of the "Caribbean of the Rockies".

Limber Pine Trail

The Limber Pine Nature Trail is a scenic 1.5-mile loop located at the summit of Logan Canyon, offering hikers a refreshing escape into a high-elevation forest of Douglas fir and Engelmann spruce. The trail's crowning jewel is a massive, 600-year-old limber pine that stands as a testament to the area's ancient natural history and serves as a spectacular focal point for visitors. With its gentle grades, informative interpretive signs, and panoramic overlooks of Bear Lake's turquoise waters, it is the perfect family-friendly adventure for those wanting to experience Utah's alpine beauty.
